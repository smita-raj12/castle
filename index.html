World-record sandcastle built in Germany. The world's tallest sandcastle has been built not on a beach but in the German industrial city of Duisburg, in the Ruhr region. Towering 16.68m (54.5ft) high, it was sculpted by an international team and shows famous landmarks, as its theme is tourism.

The Guinness Book of World Records has more than a few outlandish categories. There are, of course, the world's largest bubble gum blown (20 inches), the most layers in a sandwich (60), the longest beard (14 feet), and the world's largest sandcastle, to name a few. And it's with this last category that the world has a new reigning champion. Indeed, the Guinness Book of World Records has recently crowned this 3, 860-ton structure, rising some 55 feet in the air, as the world's newly minted largest sandcastle. Perhaps even more impressive, the location of the world's largest sandcastle is roughly 130 miles from the closest beach in the landlocked city of Duisburg, Germany. The 55-foot sandscaper, which was sculptured by a team of designers from 10 countries over a three-week period, was made to look like a medieval city. Visitors who look closely at the sandcastle will recognize iconic architecture from around the world, including the Leaning Tower of Pisa. Constructing the world's largest sandcastle is no easy feat. In fact, this is the second year in which Duisburg attempted to accomplish this record. Last year, a group attempted to create the world's largest skyscraper, but failed as the sandcastle collapsed during construction. Had they completed it, they would have been disqualified from a the Guinness Book of World Records for using materials other than just sand and water. In this year's attempt, the designers were able to complete the structure (using water and a variety of different sand types) even after their design suffered a small collapse. The sandcastle will remain on view in Duisburg through September 29.
